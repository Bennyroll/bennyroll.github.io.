Tokyo

🗾 Japan East Asia ★ 4.9 · 3,200 reviews 🕐 Best: Mar – May, Oct – Nov
Overview

Where Tradition Meets the Future

Tokyo is a city of magnificent contradictions — ancient temples tucked between gleaming skyscrapers, whispering tea ceremonies steps from thundering train stations. Japan's capital is not just a destination; it's an education in a culture so refined, so layered, that even a month feels like a preview.

From the electric chaos of Shibuya Crossing to the serene bamboo paths of Arashiyama (a short train ride to Kyoto), Tokyo rewards the curious traveler with endless discovery.

Things To Do

Must-Do Experiences

01

Watch Dawn at Tsukiji Fish Market

Arrive before 5am to witness the legendary tuna auctions (now at Toyosu) and sample the freshest sushi breakfast of your life from the outer market stalls.

Food & Culture
02

Explore Harajuku on a Sunday

Takeshita Street bursts with Japan's legendary fashion subcultures — Lolita, Gyaru, Decora and beyond. Arrive mid-morning when the street comes alive.

Fashion & Street Life
03

Day Trip to Nikko

A 2-hour train ride north brings you to ornate Edo-era shrines surrounded by cedar forests. The Toshogu Shrine complex is jaw-dropping in its gilded excess.

Day Trip · History
04

Take a Sake Tasting in Yanaka

The preserved old-town neighborhood of Yanaka feels like Edo-era Tokyo. Wander its shotengai shopping street and stop into a sake specialist bar.

Drinks · Neighborhoods
05

Teamlab Planets or Borderless

Tokyo's immersive digital art museums are unlike anything on earth. Walk through rooms of infinite light, water, and flowers that respond to your movements.

Art · Experience
Practical Tips

Before You Go

💴

Money & Payments

Japan is still largely cash-based. Get yen from a 7-Eleven ATM. IC cards like Suica work on all trains and many convenience stores.

🚇

Getting Around

The train network is world-class and intimidating. Use Google Maps, buy a Suica card, and don't be afraid to ask — locals are genuinely helpful.

🗣️

Language

English signage is excellent in central Tokyo. Learning 5–10 Japanese phrases earns immense goodwill. Google Translate's camera mode is invaluable.

🏩

Where to Stay

Stay in Shinjuku, Shibuya, or Asakusa for central access. Try a traditional ryokan for at least one night — the full experience includes yukata robes and multi-course kaiseki dinner.
