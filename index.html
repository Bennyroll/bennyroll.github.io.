THE China Medical Missionary Journal. VOL. I. MARCH 1887. No. 1. THE MEDICAL MISSIONARY ASSOCIATION OF CHINA:— ITS FUTURE WORK. By H. W. Boone, M.D. THE medical missionaries of China have great cause for thankfulness that, by the recent election they have been brought into common bonds of union and sympathy. With a Medical Association, our honored President, and a staff of Officers, we are in a condition to work together for the common good, to know and appreciate one another in a way that we never have had the chance to do before. Our union will give us that esprit de corps without which we can never do good work as a body, and our best efforts would be scattered and unsupported. Now, the youngest, as well as the oldest member of the Association can bring forward his views and experiences, and we can learn from one another. I trust that we shall have a meeting of the Association as often as the General Missionary Conference is held, and at the same time and place. We want to know one another personally. In the establishment of a Medical Journal we have taken a great step forward. Medical missionaries are sometimes looked down upon by other medical men in China, simply because they do not know them and their work. There are clever and able men among the General Practitioners of Medicine—English, French, German and American—in China, but the medical missionaries can point to men among their own numbers who are second to none as physicians, surgeons, and obstetricians, while among the lady doctors there is a zeal and an ability worthy of all admiration. In our quarterly Medical Journal, we have now, for the first time, an organ in which to express ourselves, to report upon our work, and to enable us to garner the constantly increasing mass of observations and experience for the good of our own body and of the world in general. 大清同治 壬申三月二十三日（右側刊頭） 英 四月三十一日 第一號（左側刊頭） 【本館告白】 今天下可傳之事實多矣。而湮沒不彰者比比皆是。其故何。蓋無好事者爲之記載。遂使奇聞逸事。闃然無稱。殊可嘆惜也。溯自古今以來。史記百家。載籍極博。山海地志。紀述甚詳。然所載者皆前代之遺聞。已往之故事。且篇幅浩繁。文章高古。非離經辨志。不先於文學士。不能讀也。其至於稗官小說。代有傳書。若張華志博。柳下筆記。神仙誇誕。荒怪之書。虞初爲文。韋之譚几。茲諸類均可流觀。雜玉事。或荒誕無稽。其於文。旨或晦。或是僅能助儒者之清談。未必爲雅俗所共賞。且其於時事文。則質而不俚。專則簡。而能詳。上而學士大夫。下及農工商賈。皆能通曉者。莫如新聞紙之善矣。新報之創。自西人傳於中土。間見香港、廣東等處。商賈頗便。茲仿其意。設於中外交涉之區。凡國家之政治。風俗之變遷。中外交涉之要。商賈貿易之利。一切可驚、可愕、可喜、可懼。足以啟發人之聰明者。靡不摹載。務求其真實。無妄言。使閱者明瞭易曉。不爲浮誇之詞。不速荒唐之語。此則留心時務者。於此可以周知。而由其謀生理者。於此亦不至受欺。且此補苴之作。固大有益於天下也。且夫天下。手屈指也。其事亦至繁也。由其人。又焉能不親見也。夫誰能而足。暨所周。而自致。而於其間。獻出而凡。凡可傳之事。無不通播於天下。而使自通出。而世界之賢者。亦皆不出戶庭。而知天下之善。不善。及其利害。事。雖處與利。亦可感。而志於方隅。限於知識者。漸獲涉。多焉。四方君子。進而教之。臣其不才。不遺。實有厚望焉。申報主人謹白。 【本館條例】 敢者。新聞紙之設。原欲以關切。奇聞觀望。流佈四方者也。使不事。退搜博探。以擴我見。聞。俟何資秉。聰。並足以。博其。孰是。其不。孰是。其不可徒。狗。狗子。一、稀一色。也。茲本館。特將條例。列于左。如： 一、客題贈詩賦。或雜著。新聞。志。趣。一切篇章。 一、本新報議設於上海各店寄售。每張取錢八文。各遠處。按其。每張。取錢。十文。本館躉賣。每張取錢六文。 一、如有騷人雅士。有願以短篇教者。如天下各名區。竹枝詞。及長歌。紀事。之類。概不取值。 一、如有有名言。議論。實有。係。乎國計民生。地理。利。水。源。之類者。上。關。皇朝。經濟。之。嘉。下。知。小民。稼穡。之苦。謝。新報。不。取酬。

圖 3 1888 年李提摩太 (Timothy Richard) 寫給山東同工的信件副本
copy of part of letter from Mr Richard, to Shantung brethren, dated Tientsin Sept 7 '88 "— 'But when I think of the possibilities of the Society and of Colleagues possessing so many attractive excellencies; qualified to tell very greatly on China, I feel myself very loath to break myself off to work independently. After very much thought and prayer, and sleepless nights I might add the following plan has suggested itself to me as a poor substitute for the Institution, viz: the acceptance of the Society's programme with the following modification: That Art. III of the Society's programme should read "sole charge of evangelistic work throughout at least two counties and assisted by a staff of 12 good native evangelists." That Art IV (about the bookshop) be omitted and left to some one else to do. That Art VI should read "Translation under the same conditions as the other brethren." I say sole charge simply to prevent the repetition of the scenes I witnessed in Shansi. A copastorate of half a dozen is the certain wreck of any work.'"
Timothy Richard · Letter to Shantung Brethren · Tientsin, Sept. 7, 1888
